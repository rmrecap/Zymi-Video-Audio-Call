## 1. Purpose

ZYMI is committed to providing a safe communication environment. This policy explains how users can report abuse, harassment, or policy violations, and what happens after a report is filed.

---

## 2. What Can Be Reported

The following types of violations can be reported:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Harassment** | Targeted abuse, bullying, threats, unwanted sexual advances, stalking |
| **Hate speech** | Content promoting hatred based on race, religion, gender, sexual orientation, or other protected characteristics |
| **Illegal content** | CSAM, illegal drug sales, weapons trafficking, fraud |
| **Impersonation** | Pretending to be another user, admin, or public figure |
| **Spam** | Unsolicited commercial messages, mass messaging, scams, phishing links |
| **Doxxing** | Sharing another person's private information without consent |
| **Explicit content** | Pornography, graphic violence, self-harm content |
| **Technical abuse** | Bots, scraping, unauthorized access attempts |
| **Ban evasion** | Creating a new account after being banned |

---

## 3. How to Report

### 3.1 In-App Reporting (Recommended)
1. Open the chat or message containing the violation.
2. Tap the **menu (⋮)** button.
3. Select **Report**.
4. Choose the reason from the list.
5. (Optional) Add additional context.
6. Submit.

For user profiles:
1. Go to the user's profile.
2. Tap the **menu (⋮)** button.
3. Select **Report User**.
4. Choose the reason and submit.

### 3.2 Email Reporting
If in-app reporting is unavailable or you need to provide detailed evidence, email:

- **Email**: [admin email — to be inserted]
- **Subject line**: "Abuse Report — [reason]"

Include:
- Username(s) of the person(s) involved.
- Description of the violation.
- Date and time (approximate) of the incident.
- Screenshots, logs, or other evidence.
- Your username (optional — anonymous reports are accepted).

---

## 4. Reporter Anonymity

Reports are kept confidential. Your identity as the reporter will not be shared with the reported user. Administrators will know who filed the report for accountability purposes, but this information is not disclosed to the reported party.

Anonymous reports (via email without identifying information) are accepted and investigated, but we may not be able to follow up with the reporter.

---

## 5. What Happens After a Report

### 5.1 Triage
All reports are reviewed by an administrator. The review timeline depends on severity:

| Severity | Definition | Review SLA |
|----------|------------|------------|
| **Critical** | Illegal content (CSAM, threats of violence, terrorism), immediate danger to life | Within 24 hours |
| **High** | Harassment, hate speech, doxxing, impersonation | Within 48 hours |
| **Standard** | Spam, explicit content, technical abuse | Within 7 days |
| **Low** | Minor violations, disputes, policy questions | Within 14 days |

### 5.2 Investigation
The administrator will:
1. Review the reported content and context.
2. Review the reported user's history and previous violations.
3. Gather additional evidence if needed (e.g., reviewing related messages).
4. Determine if a violation occurred.

### 5.3 Actions
If a violation is confirmed, one or more of the following actions may be taken:

| Action | Applicable To |
|--------|---------------|
| Warning sent to user | First-time minor violations |
| Content removal | Spam, explicit content, doxxing |
| Temporary ban (7 days) | Moderate violations |
| Temporary ban (30 days) | Repeated or serious violations |
| Permanent ban | Severe violations, illegal content |
| Account deletion | CSAM, repeated severe violations |
| Report to authorities | Illegal activity (CSAM, threats, fraud) |

### 5.4 Notification
The reporter will receive:
- **Confirmation**: An automated acknowledgment that the report was received.
- **Resolution**: A brief update when action is taken (or if no violation was found).

We do not share specific disciplinary actions taken against the reported user.

## 8. False Reporting

Submitting a false or malicious report is a violation of our Community Guidelines. Consequences include:

| Offense | Action |
|---------|--------|
| First false report | Warning |
| Repeated false reports | 7-day temporary ban |
| Malicious false reports (targeted harassment via reports) | Permanent ban |
